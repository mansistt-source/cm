STYLE: LUMINOUS ANIME SOLITUDE

VISUAL IDENTITY:
Create a high-detail cinematic anime style with a poetic, atmospheric mood. The world must feel vast, quiet, emotional, and slightly dreamlike. Use expansive skies, glowing clouds, distant landscapes, forests, rooftops, train interiors, shrine paths, moonlit horizons, and open natural spaces. The emotional tone is reflective, lonely, calm, and awe-focused rather than action-heavy. The color palette must rely on saturated sky blues, deep navy shadows, cyan highlights, warm sunset gold, pale cream clouds, emerald greens, and occasional soft violet night tones. Maintain strong contrast between glowing skies and dark silhouetted subjects. Texture should be clean illustrated anime with painterly backgrounds, crisp character edges, soft bloom, subtle atmospheric haze, and very light film-like grain. Avoid photorealistic skin, 3D render plasticity, dirty realism, or flat cartoon coloring.

CINEMATOGRAPHY:
Use wide cinematic compositions that make the environment larger than the subject. Favor low-angle shots looking upward into sky, rear-facing character silhouettes, wide establishing shots, dramatic horizon lines, and deep vertical framing. Camera movement should be slow, floating, and deliberate: gentle push-ins, slow crane rises, drifting lateral moves, soft parallax through grass, leaves, train windows, or foreground objects. Use wide-angle lenses for landscapes and skies, around 18mm–28mm equivalent, with occasional medium telephoto framing for intimate character moments. Depth of field should be layered: sharp central subject or architectural frame, softened foreground grass/leaves/chess pieces, and atmospheric distance fade. Composition should place characters in the lower third or center-bottom of the frame, surrounded by large sky, trees, clouds, or architecture. Use foreground framing elements such as flowers, leaves, window frames, stone paths, chess pieces, rooftops, or grass blades to create depth.

LIGHTING:
Lighting must be expressive and directional. Primary light should come from natural celestial sources: sun behind clouds, golden-hour sunlight, moonlight, glowing sky, window light, or shafts of light through trees. Use strong backlight and rim light on characters, creating silhouettes with bright outlines. Shadows should be soft but deep, especially on characters, interiors, forests, and foreground objects. Use volumetric light rays, atmospheric haze, dust particles, glowing motes, lens bloom, soft flare, and cloud glow. Day scenes should feel luminous and saturated with blue-cyan skies and warm highlights. Night scenes should use deep navy, moonlit cyan, star glow, and subtle magical light trails. Avoid harsh studio lighting, direct flash, overexposed white backgrounds, or neutral corporate lighting.

SCENE STRUCTURE:
Open scenes with a wide environmental establishing shot: sky over city, mountain valley, forest gate, train window view, moonlit horizon, or character seen from behind facing a vast scene. Start with silence, stillness, or slow environmental motion before revealing the subject. Pacing should be slow, cinematic, and contemplative, with long holds and gentle motion rather than fast cutting. Use rhythmic transitions through light, clouds, wind, leaves, window reflections, or foreground blur. Close scenes on a continuity-friendly frame: character silhouette facing the horizon, sky filling most of the frame, a glowing window view, drifting clouds, moonlit landscape, or empty path with lingering light. End frames should leave visual space for the next shot, not hard-stop on action.

SUBJECT TREATMENT:
Characters should appear calm, focused, isolated, or emotionally restrained. Frame them from behind, in silhouette, seated in thought, standing against a large sky, or partially obscured by foreground elements. Character design should use clean anime linework, controlled facial detail, sharp hair silhouettes, expressive but minimal eyes, and clothing that reads clearly against the background. Products, tools, or symbolic objects should be treated as cinematic focal points: placed in foreground, lit by rim light or window glow, surrounded by shallow depth of field, and integrated into the environment rather than presented like commercial packshots. Objects such as chess pieces, swords, bags, phones, watches, vehicles, or devices should catch small highlights and reflections while remaining secondary to mood and atmosphere. Backgrounds should not be empty; they should carry emotional scale through sky, clouds, city distance, forest density, train windows, flowers, or architectural frames.

WHAT TO AVOID:
Avoid photorealistic live-action rendering, plastic 3D anime, cheap cartoon shading, flat lighting, dull gray color grading, excessive red/orange dominance, cyberpunk neon overload, horror lighting, gritty war realism, shaky handheld camera, fast action editing, crowded chaotic compositions, meme-like expressions, exaggerated comedy poses, heavy outlines without painterly backgrounds, low-detail skies, empty backgrounds, symmetrical corporate framing, harsh flash, washed-out colors, and generic fantasy effects. Do not imitate any specific existing anime character, franchise, studio, or copyrighted scene. Preserve the style as original cinematic anime with luminous skies, emotional silence, environmental scale, and soft atmospheric depth.
